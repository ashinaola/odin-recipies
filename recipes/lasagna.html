Lasagna

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish made by layering sheets of pasta with a variety of fillings. Typically, it includes a rich meat sauce (often made with ground beef or sausage), creamy béchamel or ricotta cheese, and a generous amount of mozzarella and Parmesan cheese. The layers are baked until bubbly and golden, creating a deliciously hearty and comforting meal.

  • Lasagna noodles (about 12 sheets)
  • Ground beef (1 pound)
  • Italian sausage (optional, 1/2 pound)
  • Onion (1 medium, chopped)
  • Garlic (2-3 cloves, minced)
  • Tomato sauce (24 ounces)
  • Tomato paste (6 ounces)
  • Crushed tomatoes (28 ounces)
  • Dried oregano (1 teaspoon)
  • Dried basil (1 teaspoon)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Black pepper (to taste)
  • Ricotta cheese (15 ounces)
  • Egg (1 large)
  • Mozzarella cheese (4 cups, shredded)
  • Fresh parsley (optional, chopped for garnish)
  • Parmesan cheese (1 cup, grated)
  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cook the lasagna noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  3. Prepare the meat sauce:
    1. In a large skillet, cook the ground beef and Italian sausage over medium heat until browned.
    2. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, and cook until the onion is translucent.
    3. Stir in the tomato sauce, tomato paste, crushed tomatoes, dried oregano, dried basil, salt, and pepper.
  4. Simmer the sauce for about 30 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  5. Prepare the cheese mixture:
    1. In a bowl, combine the ricotta cheese, egg, and 2 cups of shredded mozzarella cheese. Mix well.
  6. Assemble the lasagna:
    1. Spread a thin layer of meat sauce in the bottom of a 9x13-inch baking dish.
    2. Place a layer of lasagna noodles over the sauce.
    3. Spread a layer of the ricotta cheese mixture over the noodles.
    4. Add another layer of meat sauce over the cheese mixture.
    5. Repeat the layers (noodles, cheese mixture, meat sauce) until all ingredients are used, ending with a layer of meat sauce on top.
  7. Sprinkle the remaining 2 cups of shredded mozzarella cheese and the grated Parmesan cheese over the top.
  8. Bake:
    1. Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil (to prevent the cheese from burning).
    2. Bake in the preheated oven for 25 minutes.
    3. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 25 minutes, or until the cheese is bubbly and golden brown.
  9. Allow the lasagna to rest for about 10 minutes before serving. This helps it set and makes it easier to cut into neat slices.
