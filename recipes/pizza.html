Pizza

Pizza is a beloved dish that originated in Italy and has become popular worldwide. It typically consists of a flat, round base of dough topped with various ingredients and baked until crispy and delicious.

  • Pizza dough (store-bought or homemade)
  • Tomato sauce (1/2 cup)
  • Mozzarella cheese (2 cups, shredded)
  • Olive oil (1 tablespoon)
  • Dried oregano (1 teaspoon)
  • Dried basil (1 teaspoon)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Black pepper (to taste)
  • Fresh basil leaves (optional, for garnish)
  1. Preheat the oven to 475°F (245°C). If you have a pizza stone, place it in the oven to heat up.
  2. Prepare the dough:
    • If using store-bought dough, follow the instructions on the package.
    • If making homemade dough, roll it out on a lightly floured surface to your desired thickness.
  3. Transfer the dough to a pizza peel or a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  4. Brush the dough with olive oil to prevent it from getting soggy and to add flavor.
  5. Spread the tomato sauce evenly over the dough, leaving a small border around the edges for the crust.
  6. Sprinkle the shredded mozzarella cheese evenly over the sauce.
  7. Season with dried oregano, dried basil, salt, and black pepper.
  8. Bake:
    1. Carefully transfer the pizza to the preheated oven (or onto the pizza stone if using).
    2. Bake for 10-15 minutes, or until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbly and slightly browned.
  9. Garnish (optional):
    1. Remove the pizza from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes.
    2. Sprinkle fresh basil leaves over the top for added flavor and color.
  10. Cut the pizza into slices and enjoy!
